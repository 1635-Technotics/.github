## `§ 01` — Who we are

Newtown High School sits in Elmhurst, a neighborhood where the students collectively speak dozens of languages. Every winter a chunk of them spend their nights over a CAD model, a soldering iron, and a half-built drivetrain.

Each January, **FIRST** releases the season's game; six weeks later a 120-pound robot rolls off our shop floor. In between is the part the team is really about — learning **mechanical engineering, electrical, software, and project management** against a deadline that does not move.

Since our rookie year in 2005 we've competed **every single season**, most recently at the NYC Regional inside the historic Fort Washington Avenue Armory — where you'll find us in the stands holding three-foot-tall red **16** and **35** and never quite sitting down.

## `§ 03` — The six-week build

> From kickoff crate to competition floor in about **45 days**. Same kit, same calendar, worldwide. Here's how 1635 runs it.

| Week | Phase | What happens |
|:----:|:------|:-------------|
| **W01** | `KICKOFF` | Read the rules, argue strategy. Whole team, whiteboards, kit-of-parts, pizza — leave with a plan and rough concepts. |
| **W02** | `PROTOTYPE` | Wood, zip-ties, bad ideas. The mechanisms that survive become real subsystems. |
| **W03** | `CAD LOCK` | Design the whole machine — frame, gearboxes, intake, and wiring on one assembly. |
| **W04** | `MANUFACTURE` | CNC, mill, print, repeat. Programming starts on a chassis-only practice base. |
| **W05** | `INTEGRATE` | Wire it, code it, test it. Autonomous routines run over and over against a taped field. |
| **W06** | `COMPETE` | Final tuning, driver practice, paperwork in the binder — then an early bus to the Armory. |
